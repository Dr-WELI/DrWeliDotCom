400 words (long form)

Dr Weliton Menário Costa (Dr WELI) is a Sydney-based Brazilian-born music artist and behavioural ecologist working at the intersection of music, film and science communication. Born in rural Brazil and based in Sydney, he holds a PhD from the Australian National University's School of Ecology & Evolution, where his research on eastern grey kangaroo personality and group decision-making — work in the evolutionary biology of social behaviour — forms the scientific foundation of his creative practice. His music draws on Latin, Brazilian, Afro and Middle Eastern influences, with frequent collaborations across Sydney's diaspora music communities.

In 2024, his music video Kangaroo Time won the AAAS Dance Your PhD, the contest's global top prize. Built around the chorus of his original pop song and shot in the same Australian paddocks where he studied kangaroo behaviour for his thesis, the work translated peer-reviewed behavioural ecology into a culturally resonant music release. It travelled across 250+ media outlets globally — NPR, Smithsonian Magazine, The Guardian, BBC, ABC, Globo and the Triple J network — and is now studied in Australian classrooms as an example of research translation through the arts.

In 2025, he was named in the Falling Walls Engage Top 30 worldwide for his work bridging behavioural science and public culture. The recognition came in the same year his teacher-PD program Transforming Science Into Compelling Art was shortlisted for the same prize, and his live-music program Cabra da Peste received support from Inspiring Australia ACT and ACT Science Week.

Dr WELI is the founder of WildWooHoo, a transdisciplinary creative studio with the tagline the animal kingdom - including us. The studio's pipeline includes the upcoming EP Benevolence (three songs translating climate change into reconnection and care), the in-development animated children's series Kanga-Kangaroo, a children's book line, the live performance program Cabra da Peste, an ongoing photography project, and the Kangaroo Time Kids school workshop format.

He speaks, performs, teaches and creates in English, Portuguese and Spanish. His work has been programmed by universities, public-science institutions, festivals, schools and corporate audiences across Australia, Brazil, Europe, the UK and the United States.
