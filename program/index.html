One method,
four audiences: corporate, universities, schools, and science-art partners.

Keynotes · Workshops · Consultancy · Creative productions

People remember what they experience, not what they are told. Every format on this page runs on the same five steps.Five steps · Every room · Every format

  1. Start with a real idea. Begin with something true and worth carrying, not a slogan.
  2. Find the human angle. People remember what they feel, not what they are told.
  3. Translate it. Carry the idea into music, movement, story or image, often by connecting it to another world. Most people explain; we translate.
  4. Bring people in. A room of strangers becomes a group of participants who take part, not just watch.
  5. Leave one clear thing. Close on a single idea people keep and can act on.

The full method lives at wildwoohoo.com/method/.

Profile

Brazilian born, Sydney based, building bridges between science, performance, and public culture.

WELI facilitates and designs the work. A behavioural ecologist (PhD ANU, School of Ecology & Evolution) with research on kangaroo personality and group decision-making, he builds activations, workshops, and programmes that pair peer-reviewed science with movement, music, and storytelling, for corporate teams, university cohorts, school groups, and creative partners.

The same lens powers WildWooHoo, his creative innovation studio, where research becomes culture through music videos, film, and public moments. His Kangaroo Time, the worked example the method grew from, was the AAAS Dance Your PhD Global Winner 2024 and screened globally.
