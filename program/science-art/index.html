FAQ

For institutes, cultural partners & funders

The questions partners and funders ask most.

What is the difference between a sprint, a creative lab and a residency?

A sprint workshop runs 1 to 3 days, a creative lab runs over multiple sessions, and a residency or partnership is an embedded, longer-horizon co-creation. Each is sized to your funding window.

What outputs do you deliver?

A real piece of culture, a song, a short film, a performance, a public moment or integrated media, produced through the WildWooHoo studio and released with your institution as named partner.

Can you work within our grant or funding window?

Yes. Engagements are sized from a single piece to a multi-channel campaign to fit your funding window and reach goals.

Do you provide engagement and impact data for funder acquittal?

Yes. Reportable engagement and impact data is part of the deliverable.

Who is it for?

Research institutes and university labs, cultural organisations, museums and festivals, and public-good funders and foundations.

Do you perform at science festivals or public events?

Yes. WELI performs at science festivals and public events, turning research into film, music and performance that reaches a wide audience.

Do you work as a science communication consultant?

Yes. WildWooHoo offers science communication and creative-engagement consultancy, helping institutions and funders turn research into culture that travels.

Can you run a public engagement or science engagement program?

Yes. We design public-engagement and science-engagement programs and campaigns, co-created with institutions, festivals and funders, with impact data for acquittal.
